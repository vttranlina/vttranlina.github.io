  • comment on #971 — @chibenwa, 2026-07-20 17:20

    Fixed the failing shouldRemoveDelegatedValarmRecipientAlarmWhenOrganizerCancelsEvent test — thanks @vttranlina for the reproducer.

    Root cause: a calendar object can schedule alarms for recipients that are not attendees of the event (VALARM ATTENDEE delegation). EventAlarmHandler deleted alarms with alarmEventDAO.delete(eventUid, \<calendar owner\>), i.e. only the row of the calendar owner. The delegated recipient's row — created from the very same calendar object — survived the cancellation, which is exactly what the assertion caught.

    Fix: scope alarm deletion to the calendar object rather than to its owner.

    • AlarmEventDAO.deleteByEventPath(EventUid, String eventPath) — new method, implemented in MemoryAlarmEventDAO and MongoDBAlarmEventDAO (deleteMany on eventUid + eventPath).
    • EventAlarmHandler now uses it on both deletion paths (explicit DELETE message, and the "no upcoming alarm" outcome that covers METHOD:CANCEL).

    Matching on eventPath and not on eventUid alone is deliberate: attendees hold their own copy of the event under their own calendar path, so their alarms are left untouched and removed by their own DELETE message. Two contract tests cover both sides of that.

    Tests (JAVA_HOME=java-25):

    • AlarmEventCancellationTest — 11/11 pass (was failing)
    • AlarmEvent*Test in calendar-amqp — 34/34 pass
    • MemoryAlarmEventDAOTest, MongoDBAlarmEventDAOTest — 6/6 each
    • AlarmTriggerServiceTest 13/13, AlarmScheduleTest 4/4
